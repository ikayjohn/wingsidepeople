## Introduction

Hello you!! Welcome to Wingside.

Wingside is a locally operated quick service restaurant positioned as an international franchise through our creative approach in product and brand development. Our focus as a company is to provide a combination of clean and excellent food-on-the-go at value pricing with fun packing and atmosphere using technology as the fulcrum of our operations.

Our main aim is continuous improvement in the quality of service that we deliver within the hospitality sphere.

We have a strong vision and are very focused on exponential growth and development of the company and staff alike.

As a part of the team here at Wingside, you will receive all the training you require to become efficient and effective in hospitality; because we believe that investing in our employees is investing in our customers and the immediate society at large.

We promote a work-friendly atmosphere and maintain a strong base of mutual respect and understanding.

We want you to enjoy working here and acquire good first-hand knowledge while you’re at it.

This handbook is intended as your terms and conditions of employment and should be read thoroughly because it contains a lot of very important information.

Good luck!! Here’s to the future.

## Probation Period

The probation period is intended to give new employees the opportunity to demonstrate their ability to achieve a satisfactory level of performance and to determine whether the new position meets their expectations. Wingside uses this period to evaluate employee capabilities, work habits, and overall performance.

For all new and rehired employees, a four months’ probation is mandatory to test your performance on the job. During this period, you will undergo a series of trainings that will equip you for the quality of service and hospitality we promote after your date of hire. During this time, you’ll be paid your full salary. However, you will not be able to enjoy the employee benefits provided for our regular employees.

Any significant absence will automatically extend the probation period by the length of the absence. If Wingside determines that the appointed probation period is not ample enough to thoroughly evaluate the employee's performance, the probation period may be extended for a definite period.

Depending on your performance, if satisfactory you will either be issued a letter confirming your employment; if otherwise, your services will be terminated.

After becoming a regular employee, you may also be eligible for Wingside’s other provided benefits, subject to the terms and conditions of each benefits program.

## Nature of Employment

Employment with Wingside is a voluntary action. The employee is free to resign; however, a one-month notice is required for Regular Full-Time employees, and a 2-week notice for employees on probation. Also, Wingside may terminate the employment relationship wilfully, however, not without notice or cause.

The policies in this handbook are not intended as a binding contract, nor are they to be construed to constitute contractual obligations of any kind or a contract of employment between Wingside and any of its employees.

This handbook has been developed at the discretion of management and, except for its policy of employment-at-will, may be amended or cancelled at any time, at Wingside’s sole discretion.

These provisions supersede all existing policies and practices and may not be amended or added to without the express written approval of the Management.

## Employment Categories

It is our intention at Wingside to clarify the employment definitions and classifications for employees to understand their employment status and benefit eligibility.

### Regular Full-Time

Employees in this category are those who are not in a temporary or probation status and are regularly scheduled to work a full-time schedule. Generally, they are eligible for Wingside’s benefit package, subject to the terms, conditions, and limitations of each benefit program.

### Probation

Employees in this category are those whose performance is being evaluated to determine whether further employment in a specific position or with Wingside is appropriate. Employees who satisfactorily complete the probation period will be notified of their new employment status.

### Contractual

Employees in this category are those who are hired as transitional replacements, to temporarily supplement the work force, or to assist in the completion of a specific project. Employments of these nature are for a limited time. Employment beyond any initially stated period does not in any way imply a change in employment status.

Temporary employees retain that status unless and until notified of a change. Temporary employees are ineligible for all Wingside’s benefit programs.

## Performance Evaluation

Supervisors and employees are strongly encouraged to discuss job performance and goals on an informal, day-to-day basis. Additional formal performance evaluations are conducted to provide both supervisors and employees the opportunity to discuss job tasks, identify and correct weaknesses, encourage and recognize strengths, and discuss positive, purposeful approaches for meeting goals.

After the employee has completed his probation period of 4 months, the immediate supervisor evaluates the performance of the employee and makes necessary adjustments if necessary.

## Employee Relations

If employees have concerns about work conditions or compensation, they are strongly encouraged to voice these concerns openly and directly to their supervisors.

Our experience has shown that when employees deal openly and directly with supervisors, the work environment can be excellent, communications can be clear, and attitudes can be positive. We believe that Wingside amply demonstrates its commitment to employees by responding effectively to employee concerns.

In an effort to protect and maintain direct employer/employee communications, we will do anything we can to protect the right of employees to speak for themselves.

The objective is to consistently engender positive energy between team members and management.

## Personal Relationships in the Workplace

The employment of relatives or individuals involved in a dating relationship in the same area of an organization may cause serious conflicts and problems with favoritism and employee morale. In addition to claims of partiality in treatment at work, personal conflicts from outside the work environment can be carried over into day-to-day working relationships.

For purposes of this policy, a relative is any person who is related by blood or marriage, or whose relationship with the employee is similar to that of persons who are related by blood or marriage. A dating relationship is defined as a relationship that may be reasonably expected to lead to the formation of a consensual romantic or sexual relationship.

This policy applies to all employees without regard to the gender or sexual orientation of the individuals involved.

Although Wingside has no prohibition against employing relatives of current employees or individuals involved in a dating relationship with current employees, we are committed to monitoring situations in which such relationships exist in the same area. Employees are obligated to inform Wingside about the existence of such relationships.

In cases of actual or potential problems, Wingside will take prompt action, and this can include reassignment or termination. Employees in a close personal relationship should refrain from public workplace displays of affection or excessive personal conversation.

## Conflicts of Interest

Employees have an obligation to conduct business within guidelines that prohibit actual or potential conflicts of interest.

An actual or potential conflict of interest occurs when an employee is in a position to influence a decision that may result in a personal gain for that employee or for a relative because of Wingside business dealings.

No presumption of guilt is created by the mere existence of a relationship with outside firms. However, if employees have any influence on transactions involving purchases, contracts, or leases, they must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest as soon as possible.

Personal gain may result not only in cases where an employee or relative has significant ownership in a firm with which Wingside does business, but also when an employee or relative receives any kickback, bribe, substantial gift, or special consideration.

## Outside Employment

Employees may hold outside jobs if they meet the performance standards of their job with Wingside.

All employees will be judged by the same performance standards and will be subject to Wingside scheduling demands, regardless of any existing outside work requirements.

Outside employment that constitutes a conflict of interest is prohibited. Employees may not receive any income or material gain from individuals outside Wingside for materials produced or services rendered while performing their jobs.

## Non-Disclosure

The protection of confidential business information and trade secrets is vital to the interests and success of Wingside.

Confidential information includes but is not limited to:

- Compensation data
- Customer lists
- Customer preferences
- Financial information
- Marketing strategies
- Pending projects and proposals
- Research and development strategies
- Food and drink recipes

Management and kitchen staff are required to sign a non-disclosure agreement as a condition of employment.

Employees who improperly use or disclose trade secrets or confidential business information will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment and legal action.

## Working Hours

Your days of work will be 7 days in a week with two day off. Due to the nature of the business your hours of work will depend on a time shift (morning/evening), consisting of eight hours per day.

Your job will include working unsocial hours, including evenings (depending on your shift), and all Public Holidays.

During working hours you must not publicly answer phone calls or chats, chat with customers/colleagues or loiter.

## Pay

You will be paid the agreed salary in your bank account as included in your contract of employment.

If your personal details change, please inform your immediate supervisor as soon as possible.

## Paid Leave

8 months after confirmation of employment, excluding all normal Public Holidays, employees are entitled to paid leave.

Specifications by role:

- Senior Management: 4 weeks
- Line Management: 3 weeks
- Others: 3 weeks

## Bereavement Leave

Employees who require taking time off due to the death of an immediate family member should notify their supervisor immediately.

Paid bereavement leave will be provided to employees having worked 60 calendar days for Wingside.

This includes:

- Five (5) working days in the case of the death of an employee's spouse, child or the employee's spouse's child.
- Three (3) working days in the case of the death of an employee's father, mother, sister or brother.
- One (1) working day in specified extended-family cases as defined by company policy.

Bereavement pay is calculated based on the base pay rate at the time of absence and will not include incentives, commissions, bonuses, or shift differentials.

## Absences from Work

Should you be unable to come to work for whatever reason you must inform us at your earliest convenience.

We require at least twelve hours’ notice of intended absence so we can arrange cover.

When informing us, include reason and likely duration of absence.

## Absences Through Sickness

Should you be absent from work due to illness for more than 3 days, we require a doctor’s report to verify your illness and enable necessary payments.

## Marriage, Maternity and Parental Leave

Any of our female employees who are pregnant will have their statutory rights to ante-natal care, maternity pay, and maternity leave met accordingly.

### Marriage

One (1) paid working day off is allowed for the marriage of the employee or one of his/her children.

### Maternity Leave Admissibility

Current Government legislation entitles employees to a combined Maternity/Parental leave, without pay, of up to 52 weeks.

Employees that wish to benefit from Parental Leave only are entitled to a leave of absence, without pay, of up to 37 weeks.

### Birth of a Child or Adoption

There will be two (2) paid working days off during the birth of the employee’s child or adoption of a child (leave of paternity).

Violation of company policy may result in discipline including demotion, suspension, or termination.

Wingside may apply progressive discipline but is not required to do so.

## Progressive Disciplinary & Grievance Procedures

The disciplinary procedure may be entered at any stage depending on seriousness.

### Contents of Warnings

Any warning includes:

1. Reason for warning.
2. Action required for improvement.
3. Timescale for improvement.
4. Consequences of further offences.
5. Right to appeal.

### Verbal Warnings

Issued for minor first offences; typically retained in file for 1 month subject to improvement.

### First Written Warnings

Issued for continued faults or serious incidents; retained for 1 month subject to improvement.

### Final Written Warnings

Issued for continued faults or serious offences short of gross misconduct; retained for 3 months subject to improvement.

### Gross Misconduct

Examples include theft, fraud, serious insubordination, endangering safety, violence, intoxication on duty, serious negligence, serious health and safety breaches, arson, bullying, harassment or discrimination.

### Dismissal

After continued misconduct and completion of procedure up to final warning, dismissal may follow disciplinary hearing.

### Appeals

Appeals must be made in writing within 7 days of disciplinary letter.

### Grievance Procedure

Concerns should be raised with immediate supervisor first, then escalated in writing if unresolved.

## Reporting Violations
